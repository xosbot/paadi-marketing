Paadi Veedu Kitchen

The 51-Item Feast — An Ethnic Restaurant Experience

Not a canteen. Not a buffet. A traditional Kerala ethnic restaurant at Ezhikkara where 51 households cook one item each and serve it on banana leaves — an experience that can only happen here.

An Experience, Not a Meal

Paadi Veedu Kitchen (പാടി വീട് അടുക്കള)

Paadi Veedu Kitchen is a dedicated ethnic restaurant built in traditional Kerala architectural style — thatched roof, open-air dining, banana-leaf service, a visible traditional kitchen — where the recurring 51-item Pokkali feast comes alive every week.

Each of the 51 dishes is cooked in an individual host household, then assembled and served at the restaurant. The tourist doesn't just eat — they experience a village feeding its guests, with recipes passed down for generations.

Capacity: 40–60 guests per seating
Frequency: Weekly (pilot) → bi-weekly → weekly at scale
Price: ₹800–₹1,200 per person
Run by: Women's Self-Help Groups (SHGs)

🍛

51 Dishes

Each from a different household — rice, prawn curry, fish thoran, banana-leaf sadya items, payasams, pickles, kappa, fermented rice preparations

👩‍🍳

SHG-Run

Women's Self-Help Groups coordinate the feast, kitchen standards, and FSSAI-compliant food safety

🌿

Zero Waste

Only steel, glass, banana leaf, and areca palm plates. Kitchen waste composted on-site. No single-use plastic
