# Pokkali Village — 6-Month Marketing Plan

**Prepared for:** Palliyakkal Service Cooperative Bank / PAADI
**Date:** June 2026
**Duration:** Month 1 – Month 6 (July – December 2026)

---

## Executive Summary

This plan transforms Pokkali Village into **Kerala's premier eco-tourism destination**, driving revenue across four verticals: **village tourism, eco-products, hospitality, and food business.**

**Core Strategy:** Build digital presence, establish tour operations, launch e-commerce, and scale through partnerships — anchored by a 3,000-year-old, zero-chemical, GI-tagged rice-prawn farming system, Kerala's first electric backwater boat, a 51-dish community kitchen, and live Organic Theater performances.

**6-Month Targets:** 1,000+ visitors, 200+ foreign tourists, 15,000+ IG followers, 100+ TripAdvisor reviews, 5+ partner operators, 5+ press features.

## 13. Pricing Strategy

### 13.1 Product Architecture

Five core packages, positioned as **"premium affordable"** — more expensive than Kumbalangi (signals quality), cheaper than Thekkady (signals accessibility).

| Package | Duration | Price (per person) | Includes |
|---------|----------|-------------------|----------|
| **Pokkali Discovery** | 4 hours | ₹1,500 – ₹2,500 | Farm walk, canoe ride, Paadi Veedu Kitchen meal, farmer chat |
| **Overnight Farm Stay** | 24 hours | ₹4,000 – ₹6,000 | Stay, electric boat cruise, sunset canoe, cooking class, Paadi Veedu Kitchen lunch, campfire |
| **3-Day Regenerative Retreat** | 3D/2N | ₹12,000 – ₹18,000 | Farming, conservation, kayaking, Ayurveda, birdwatching, electric boat cruise, Paadi Veedu Kitchen feast, Organic Theater performance, all meals |
| **Pokkali Cooking Class** | 3 hours | ₹1,000 – ₹1,500 | Hands-on rice + prawn cookery, recipe card, lunch at Paadi Veedu Kitchen |
| **Corporate CSR Outing** | 1 day | ₹3,000 – ₹5,000/pp | Team-building, wetland restoration, group cooking, boat race, Paadi Veedu Kitchen group dining |
| **Water Sports Package** | 3–4 hours | ₹2,000 – ₹3,500 | Electric boat backwater cruise, kayaking, canoe ride, PAADI Tales heritage tour, light refreshments at jetty |

*Key experience elements across all packages:* Paadi Veedu Kitchen (community feast), Water Sports Jetty (electric boat, kayaking, canoe), Organic Theater (Kadamban Moothan's weekly performance), plus farm walks, cooking classes, and farmer interaction.

### 13.2 Add-Ons & Upsells

### 13.4 Dynamic Pricing Rules

- **Peak season (Nov–Feb):** +20% on all packages
- **Monsoon (Jun–Sep):** -20% to drive volume; reframe as "wellness + Ayurveda monsoon retreat"
- **Weekend uplift (Fri–Sun):** +10% on day tours
- **Group discount:** 15% off for groups of 8+ (corporate + family)
- **Founding visitor rate (Month 1):** -20% flat, lifetime "Pokkali Pioneer" badge in return for honest review
- **Foreign tourist (non-INR card):** rate held in INR; no surcharge but 3% gateway fee absorbed

### 13.5 Channel Commission Model

## 14. Competitor Playbook

> *Per-competitor counter-positioning — what we say, where we show up, what we don't do.*

### 14.1 Direct Competitors

#### **Pizhala Pokkali Farm** (Kadamakudy, 2–3 rooms, no digital)
- **Their wedge:** Already operating, authentic, foreign-tourist repeat
- **Our counter:** Scale + digital + bank backing
- **What we say (publicly):** "Pokkali Village is the home of every Pokkali farmer — Pizhala is our cousin village, and we send overflow guests to them when we are full"
- **What we do:** Don't attack; position as part of the same ecosystem. Co-list on tour operator packages. Cross-promote. Their weakness (no digital) is our marketing flywheel — every Pizhala guest who Googles "Pokkali" should find us first via SEO + YouTube.
- **What we don't do:** Don't poach their guides; don't discount against their rates; don't badmouth on social.

#### **Cochin Magic "Story of a Rice"** (Kochi periphery, tour-only)
- **Their wedge:** Professional guide, polished narrative
- **Our counter:** Own the experience end-to-end (stay + food + farm) — plus Paadi Veedu Kitchen, Electric Boat, and Organic Theater that they don't have
- **What we say:** "Story of a Rice" is a great intro — Pokkali Village is the full day, the overnight, the harvest"
- **What we do:** Hire a more senior guide than theirs; offer the overnight that they can't.

#### **Blue Yonder Ezhikkara Tours** (Ezhikkara, small, inconsistent)
- **Their wedge:** Local storytelling, Blue Yonder brand credibility
- **Our counter:** Reliability + standardisation + Ezhikkara-resident legitimacy + Paadi Veedu Kitchen + Electric Boat
- **What we do:** Approach Blue Yonder founder for partnership, not fight. Offer to be their "Pokkali experience" white-label partner for premium groups. They get reliable ops; we get their brand reach.

#### **Spice Village / Pepper County (Thekkady)**
- **Their wedge:** International brand recognition, luxury positioning
- **Our counter:** Half-day proximity from Kochi vs. 4-hour drive; participatory, not passive
- **What we say:** "Same Kerala, half the drive, twice the story"
- **What we do:** Package with Fort Kochi stay as a 2-night Kochi-Pokkali combo to compete on itinerary, not just price.

### 14.2 Indirect Competitors

#### **Kumbalangi Model Tourism Village**
- **Their wedge:** RT Mission-backed, near Kochi, well-known
- **Our counter:** GI rice story they can't replicate; overnight stays; Paadi Veedu Kitchen community feast; Organic Theater; electric boat
- **What we say:** "Kumbalangi is a fishing village. Pokkali is a farming civilisation."

#### **Kumarakom**
- **Their wedge:** Established, houseboat combo, premium
- **Our counter:** Authentic, not commercialised; food + farm focus over backwater; electric boat = silent backwater vs. diesel houseboat
- **What we say:** "Kumarakom is the polished Kerala. Pokkali is the real Kerala."

#### **Thenmala / Wayanad farm stays**
- **Their wedge:** Adventure + nature
- **Our counter:** Closer to Kochi, deeper story
- **What we say:** "Three hours shorter, three thousand years deeper."

### 14.3 What We Will Never Do

- **No corporate-sponsored "experience" deals** that compromise authenticity (e.g., Colgate-Pokkali "smiles in the fields")
- **No group sizes above 20/day** in core fields; split groups, rotate
- **No discounting below the floor (₹1,200/person)** even in low season
- **No staged "tribal" or "village dance" performances** — only real festivals and Kadamban Moothan's Organic Theater (community-owned, agriculturally themed)
- **No copycat content from competitors** — if you can find it on Pizhala's Instagram, don't post it

### 14.4 Monthly Competitor Watch

- Track 5 competitors on Google Alerts + Mention.com (free tier)
- Quarterly review: pricing, packages, new offerings, press hits
- Owner: Marketing Lead; 30-min monthly check

## 19. Crisis Communications Plan

> *When something goes wrong, what do we say, where, and how fast?*

### 19.1 Crisis Categories & Response

| Crisis Type | Severity | Response Time | Owner |
|-------------|----------|---------------|-------|
| **Visitor injury (minor)** | M | 1 hour internal; same-day public if reported | Ops Lead |
| **Visitor injury (serious/hospital)** | H | 30 min internal; 2-hour public statement | Chairman + Marketing Lead |
| **Food safety incident** | H | 1 hour internal; 4-hour public; FSSAI notification | Ops + Marketing |
| **Negative viral review (unfounded)** | M | 6-hour public response; offer private resolution | Marketing Lead |
| **Negative viral review (legitimate)** | H | 2-hour public apology; 24-hour action plan | Chairman + Marketing |
| **Monsoon/flood damage** | H | 4-hour safety update; pause bookings; phased reopen | Ops Lead |
| **Farmer-community dispute** | H | 24-hour internal; quiet resolution; no public unless escalated | Chairman + Community Coordinator |
| **Copycat / IP theft** | M | Legal notice within 48 hours; public statement if material | Marketing + Legal |
| **Media ambush / hit piece** | H | 2-hour holding statement; full reply within 24 hours | Chairman + Marketing Lead |
| **Cyber (IG hacked, site defaced)** | H | 1-hour containment; 6-hour public update if user data at risk | Marketing + Tech |

### 19.2 Holding Statement Templates

**Generic (any incident):**
> "We are aware of [incident] and are taking it seriously. The safety of our visitors and community is our top priority. We are gathering facts and will share an update within [X hours]. For urgent inquiries: [contact]."

**Injury/medical:**
> "A visitor at Pokkali Village received medical attention for [X] on [date]. They are [stable/recovering]. We are cooperating fully with [authorities] and have launched an internal review. We will share further updates as verified information becomes available."

**Food safety:**
> "We have been made aware of a concern regarding food served at Pokkali Village on [date]. We have voluntarily paused [item/service] pending an internal food safety audit. No other guests are affected. We will share results within 24 hours."

**Negative review (acknowledging fault):**
> "We hear [guest name] and we're sorry. What they experienced does not match the experience we want to deliver. We've reached out privately to make it right and are reviewing [specific process] to prevent this from happening again. Thank you for holding us accountable."

### 19.3 Channel-Specific Protocols

- **Owned channels (IG/FB/website):** Update within 6 hours for H-severity; 24 hours for M
- **WhatsApp broadcasts to booked guests:** Within 2 hours if they're affected
- **Email to full list:** Within 24 hours for any H-severity; only if material
- **Press:** Reactive only; no proactive press unless it escalates publicly
- **Google/TripAdvisor response:** Within 24 hours, even on 1-star reviews

### 19.4 Pre-Built Crisis Kit (To Be Assembled in Month 1)

- [ ] Crisis contact tree (10 names, mobile, role)
- [ ] Designated spokesperson (PAADI Chairman; backup: Marketing Lead)
- [ ] Holding statement templates (above) — saved in shared drive
- [ ] Pre-cleared photo + video library for "normal day at Pokkali" (counter-narrative)
- [ ] Insurance + legal counsel contact (M/s [TBD], Ernakulam)
- [ ] Media monitoring alerts set up (Google Alerts: "Pokkali Village", "Ezhikkara tourism")
- [ ] Quarterly tabletop drill (Q2 and Q4)

### 19.5 Things We Will Never Do in a Crisis

- **Delete or hide negative comments** (respond; don't erase)
- **Blame the customer** (even if facts are on our side — public first, private resolution)
- **Go silent for >24 hours** (silence is the worst narrative)
- **Comment on legal threats publicly** (private channel only)
- **Get into Twitter/X fights** (one calm statement, then off the platform)
